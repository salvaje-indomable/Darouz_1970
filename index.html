Our story

Three generations,
one counter.

Darouz was never just a sandwich shop. It was a promise — to show up every day, to feed your neighbors, to keep the table set. Since 1970, that promise has been passed from hand to hand in Latakia, Syria.

The founder — Latakia, 1970
1970
Where it all began

It started with one man, a small counter, and a belief that a great sandwich could make someone's day. Our grandfather opened the first Darouz in Latakia with fresh ingredients, family recipes, and an open door.

A neighborhood institution

The shop became a gathering place. Friends met here before school, workers stopped in before shifts, families made it part of their rhythm. The recipes stayed the same. The welcome always did too.

Behind the counter — early days
Second generation, carrying it forward
The next generation picks up the knife

When the time came, the shop didn't close — it grew. The second generation stepped behind the counter with the same dedication and recipes. Same bread. Same spirit. Same love for every customer.

Fresh every morning, always

Every day starts the same way at Darouz — fresh ingredients laid out and ready. The labneh, the cheeses, the herbs. Nothing premade, nothing yesterday's. This is the standard we set in 1970 and have never lowered.

Made fresh, every single day
Today — the legacy continues
Still here. Still family.

More than fifty years on, Darouz is still run by the same family. The counter looks a little different now — warmer lights, a neon sign glowing amber into the street. But behind it stands someone who grew up eating these sandwiches.

"Where every corner holds a piece of our childhood."

— Darouz, since 1970
